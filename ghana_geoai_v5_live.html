📜 Regulatory Framework
  • Minerals & Mining Act 2006 (Act 703) — governing legislation
  • Minerals Commission — licensing and regulatory body
  • 5% royalty on gross value of minerals produced
  • 1% of gross revenue to Social Responsibility Fund (SRF)
  • Environmental bond required before operations commence
  • JORC/NI 43-101 compliant resource reporting mandatory
  • EIA required under EPA Act 490 before surface disturbance
  • Mining lease maximum 30 years, renewable

  • Winneba Formation — coastal manganese nodules

        Mining Formulas Calculator
        All key formulas for gold and mineral mining in Ghana — JORC, economic, geotechnical, and regulatory
        JORC Ore Resource Tonnage
        Estimates total tonnes of ore using block model volume, density, and classification factors. Used for Inferred, Indicated, and Measured resources.
        T = V × ρ × CF
        T = Tonnes of ore
        V = Block volume (m³)
        ρ = Ore density (t/m³)
        CF = Classification factor (0.8–1.0)
        Result: —
        Contained Metal (Gold Ounces)
        Converts ore tonnes and grade into contained metal ounces — the primary JORC resource reporting metric.
        M = (T × G) / 31.1035
        M = Contained metal (troy oz)
        T = Ore tonnes
        G = Average grade (g/t)
        31.1035 = grams per troy oz
        Result: —
        Cutoff Grade
        The minimum grade of ore that can be profitably mined given operating costs, gold price, and recovery. Critical for JORC reporting.
        COG = (C_op) / (P_Au × R × 31.1035)
        COG = Cutoff grade (g/t)
        C_op = Operating cost ($/t)
        P_Au = Gold price ($/oz)
        R = Metallurgical recovery (%)
        Result: —
        Taylor's Rule — Mine Production Rate
        Taylor's empirical rule estimates optimum annual throughput based on total ore reserves. Widely used in Ghana for feasibility studies.
        Q = 0.2 × (Reserves)^0.75
        Q = Annual production rate (Mt/yr)
        Reserves = Total ore reserves (Mt)
        Mine life ≈ Reserves / Q (years)
        Result: —
        Net Present Value (NPV)
        Core mine valuation metric. Determines present value of all future cash flows at a discount rate. Used by all major Ghana mining companies.
        NPV = Σ [CF_t / (1+r)^t] - I₀
        CF_t = Cash flow in year t
        r = Discount rate
        t = Time period (years)
        I₀ = Initial capital investment
        Result: —
        All-In Sustaining Cost (AISC)
        The World Gold Council standard metric for true cost of gold production. Includes all operating, sustaining capital, and royalty costs.
        AISC = (OpEx + SustCapEx + Royalty + G&A) / Au_oz
        OpEx = Direct mining & processing costs
        SustCapEx = Sustaining capital
        Royalty = 5% of gross revenue (Ghana)
        G&A = General & Administrative costs
        Result: —
        Revenue Projection
        Annual mine revenue based on production rate, grade, recovery, and spot gold price.
        Rev = T × G × R × P_Au / 31.1035
        T = Annual tonnes mined
        G = Head grade (g/t)
        R = Recovery rate
        P_Au = Gold price ($/oz)
        Result: —
        Payback Period
        Simple payback period for a mining investment — critical metric for junior miners in Ghana seeking project finance.
        PBP = CapEx / Annual Net CF
        Annual Net CF = Revenue - OpEx - Royalty - Tax
        Ghana CIT = 35% on mining income
        Royalty = 5% gross revenue
        Result: —
        CIL/CIP Gold Recovery
        Carbon-In-Leach / Carbon-In-Pulp process recovery — the dominant gold processing method in Ghana's oxide and transitional ore zones.
        R = (Au_in - Au_out) / Au_in × 100%
        Au_in = Feed grade × feed tonnes
        Au_out = Tailings grade × tailings tonnes
        Ghana CIL recovery typically 85–92%
        Result: —
        Mill Throughput — Bond Work Index
        Bond Work Index formula estimates energy required to grind ore to target product size. Critical for mill design in Ghana's hard-rock mines.
        W = Wi × (10/√P₈₀ - 10/√F₈₀)
        W = Specific energy (kWh/t)
        Wi = Bond Work Index (kWh/t)
        P₈₀ = Product 80% passing size (µm)
        F₈₀ = Feed 80% passing size (µm)
        Result: —
        Stripping Ratio
        Ratio of waste rock to ore in an open-pit mine. Determines the economic viability of open-cut vs underground mining at a Ghana site.
        SR = Waste / Ore (tonnes or m³)
        SR < 5:1 → favourable open pit
        SR > 8:1 → consider underground
        Break-even SR = (Value - OpEx) / Waste cost
        Result: —
        Dilution Factor
        Percentage of waste rock unintentionally included in ore mining. Critical for reconciliation between resource model and plant feed in Ghana mines.
        D = (Ore_mined - Ore_model) / Ore_mined × 100%
        Typical underground dilution: 10–25%
        Open pit dilution: 5–12%
        Adjusted Grade = Model Grade × (1 - D%)
        Result: —
        Drill Hole Density for Resources
        Required drill spacing for JORC resource classification in Ghana. Typically RC (Reverse Circulation) or DD (Diamond Drilling).
        Measured: spacing ≤ 25m × 25m
        Indicated: spacing ≤ 50m × 50m
        Inferred: spacing ≤ 100m × 100m
        Area coverage = L × W / (spacing²)
        Result: —
        Sample Quality — QAQC
        QAQC blanks, duplicates, and standards ratio for Ghana Minerals Commission compliant sampling programmes.
        Standards: 1 per 20 samples (5%)
        Blanks: 1 per 20 samples (5%)
        Duplicates: 1 per 10 samples (10%)
        Total QAQC = ~20% of programme
        Result: —
        True Width from Drill Intercept
        Converts drill hole intersection length to true vein/lode width. Essential for correct resource estimation in Ghana's steeply dipping shear zones.
        TW = L × cos(α - β)
        TW = True width
        L = Drilled interval length
        α = Dip of drill hole (°)
        β = Dip of ore body (°)
        Result: —
        Composite Grade Calculation
        Length-weighted composite of drill hole sample grades — standard for preparing input to block model grade estimation in Ghana mines.
        G_comp = Σ(g_i × l_i) / Σ(l_i)
        g_i = Sample grade (g/t)
        l_i = Sample length (m)
        Minimum composite = 2m for Au
        Result: —
        Ghana Mineral Royalty
        Under the Minerals & Mining Act 2006 (Act 703), all mineral producers pay royalty to Ghana government based on gross revenue.
        Royalty = 5% × Gross Revenue
        SRF = 1% × Gross Revenue
        Total govt take = 6% of gross
        Annual Revenue = Oz × Au Price
        Result: —
        Corporate Income Tax — Mining
        Ghana mining companies are taxed at 35% CIT under Income Tax Act 2015 (Act 896). First 5 years may qualify for stability agreement.
        CIT = 35% × (Revenue - AllowableDeductions)
        Allowable: OpEx, depreciation, royalty
        Capital allowance: 80% in Year 1
        Ring-fence: per mining right area
        Result: —
        Environmental Bond Estimate
        Ghana EPA requires an environmental bond equal to estimated reclamation cost before mining commences. Calculated per disturbed hectare.
        Bond = DisturbedArea × ReclamationCost
        Typical reclamation: $8,000–$25,000/ha
        Tailing dam: $15,000–$40,000/ha
        Annual progressive rehab required
        Result: —
        Local Content Compliance
        Ghana's Minerals & Mining (General) Regulations 2012 require local procurement and employment quotas at all mining operations.
        Local employment: ≥90% Ghanaian staff
        Local procurement: ≥30% of goods/services
        Local shareholding: ≥10% Ghanaian equity
        GNPC share: negotiable per licence
        Result: —
        Factor of Safety — Pit Slope
        Critical for open-pit mine design in Ghana's laterite-covered greenstone terrains. Minimum FS = 1.3 for static, 1.0 for seismic conditions.
        FS = (c + σ tanφ) / τ
        c = Cohesion (kPa)
        σ = Normal stress (kPa)
        φ = Friction angle (°)
        τ = Shear stress (kPa)
        Result: —
        Rock Mass Rating (RMR)
        Bieniawski's Rock Mass Rating system classifies rock quality for underground mining support design in Ghana's deep gold mines.
        RMR = R1+R2+R3+R4+R5+R6
        R1: UCS (0–15 pts)
        R2: RQD (3–20 pts)
        R3: Joint spacing (5–20 pts)
        R4: Joint condition (0–30 pts)
        R5: Groundwater (0–15 pts)
        R6: Orientation correction
        Result: —
        Ventilation — Minimum Airflow
        Minimum fresh air requirement for underground mines in Ghana under Minerals & Mining (Health, Safety and Technical) Regulations.
        Q_min = 0.06 m³/s per kW of diesel
        Q_workers = 0.03 m³/s per person
        Velocity: 0.3 – 4.0 m/s in drives
        Dilution factor × DPM concentration
        Result: —
        Tailing Dam Capacity
        Estimates total tailing storage volume required over mine life. Critical for EPA bond calculation and dam engineering design in Ghana.
        V = T_annual × Mine_life / (ρ_dry × CF)
        T_annual = Annual mill throughput (t/yr)
        ρ_dry = Dry tails density (t/m³)
        CF = Void fill factor (~0.65)

        Result: —
